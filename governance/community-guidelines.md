PhilaCon Valley is built on a simple promise: everyone who walks through the door — physically or digitally — belongs here. These guidelines exist to protect that promise. They apply everywhere the community gathers: in-person events, online channels, collaborative projects, and any space that carries the PhilaCon Valley name.

This is not a legal document or a list of punishments. It is a shared agreement about how we treat each other — and what happens when that agreement is broken.

These guidelines are built on the [values](../foundation/community-values.md) and [principles](../foundation/principles.md) already at the heart of this community. If those documents describe who we are, this one describes how we live it out — especially when things get complicated.

---

## The Short Version

Be generous with your energy. Be honest without being cruel. Make space for people who have less of it. If you see something wrong, say something. If you mess up, own it.

---

## Expected Behavior

**Show up with respect.** Treat every person in this community — regardless of their skill level, background, identity, or role — with the same respect you'd want in return. Respect means listening when someone speaks, not talking over people, and recognizing that everyone is here to grow.

**Make space, take space.** If you tend to talk a lot, practice leaving room. If you tend to stay quiet, know that your voice matters and we want to hear it. Good communities are built by people who pay attention to the room, not just their own experience.

**Assume good intent, but address impact.** When someone says something that lands wrong, the intention behind it doesn't erase the impact. We believe in addressing harm directly and compassionately — not brushing it off because someone "didn't mean it."

**Honor people's identities.** Use the names and pronouns people ask you to use. Don't make assumptions about anyone's gender, sexuality, race, ability, or background. If you get it wrong, correct yourself and move on — no big performance, just basic respect.

**Keep it accessible.** Whether you're presenting, writing, or sharing a resource — think about who might be left out. Use plain language when you can. Describe images. Consider physical accessibility. Inclusion isn't a feature; it's a practice.

**Protect the space.** PhilaCon Valley was built for Black, Brown, LGBTQIA+, and underrepresented technologists. That doesn't mean others aren't welcome — it means this community's priorities are clear. If you're here as an ally, follow before you lead.

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors will not be tolerated in any PhilaCon Valley space:

**Harassment.** Unwelcome comments about someone's identity, appearance, or background. Deliberate intimidation. Stalking or following. Unwanted photography or recording. Sustained disruption of events or discussions. Any form of unwelcome physical contact or sexual attention. None of this has a place here.

**Discrimination.** Exclusionary behavior or language that targets someone based on who they are — race, ethnicity, gender, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, disability, age, religion, national origin, immigration status, socioeconomic background, or any other identity. We built this space to counter exclusion, not reproduce it.

**Aggression.** Threats of violence, incitement to violence, or deliberate intimidation of any kind. This includes "jokes" that target marginalized groups. If the punchline is someone's identity, it's not funny — it's harm.

**Boundary violations.** Continuing any behavior after someone has asked you to stop. "No" is a complete sentence. Respect it.

**Bad-faith participation.** Showing up primarily to recruit, sell, promote, or extract value from the community without contributing to it. We can tell the difference between someone who is here to build and someone who is here to take.

**Doxxing or privacy violations.** Sharing someone's personal information without their explicit consent — photos, contact details, employment information, or anything shared in confidence.

**Retaliation.** Punishing, excluding, or targeting anyone who reports a concern or participates in a resolution process. Reporting takes courage. We protect that.

## In Online Spaces

Our messaging channels, event pages, and social platforms are extensions of our in-person community — not separate from it. The same expectations apply, plus a few that are specific to how digital spaces work:

**Don't spam.** Keep self-promotion to designated channels. Sharing something you built or wrote is fine; turning every thread into a pitch is not. If you're unsure whether something counts as spam, it probably does.

**Engage in good faith.** Disagreement is welcome — bad-faith arguments, trolling, and pile-ons are not. Critique ideas, not people. If a thread is getting heated, step back before you type.

**Be mindful of tone.** Text strips away nuance. The same sentence that sounds fine in person can read as aggressive in a message. When in doubt, assume the best of each other and ask clarifying questions before reacting.

**Respect people's time and attention.** Don't DM people unsolicited sales pitches, job offers, or requests for free labor. If you want to connect with someone, do it in shared channels first — that way it's a conversation, not a cold approach.

**Keep channels on-topic.** Use the right channels for the right conversations. Off-topic messages make it harder for people to find what they need, and over time they erode the usefulness of the space.

**No hate speech.** Slurs, dehumanizing language, and content that targets any identity group — there's no version of this that's acceptable here. It will result in immediate removal.

## At Events

Our events are built to be spaces of belonging. That takes everyone's participation:

**Arrive ready to engage.** You don't have to be the most outgoing person in the room — but show up with an open mind and a willingness to participate. These events are for active members, not passive audiences.

**Respect the format.** If it's a workshop, follow along. If it's a discussion, listen as much as you speak. If it's a build night, build. The format exists for a reason.

**Handle disagreements with care.** Passionate disagreement about ideas is welcome. Personal attacks are not. If a conversation gets heated, take a breath, check your assumptions, and remember you're talking to a person — not an argument.

**Be mindful of shared space.** Clean up after yourself. Be aware of noise levels. If you're in a group, make room for newcomers. Small courtesies add up.

**Ask before you photograph or record.** Not everyone wants to be on camera. Ask first. Respect the answer.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates these guidelines, we want to know. You have several options:

**Talk to an organizer directly.** At in-person events, organizers will be identifiable and approachable. Pull one aside — this is exactly what we're here for.

**Send a message.** If you'd rather not report in person, reach out to the organizer team through our designated reporting channel. Details will be shared in onboarding materials and pinned in online spaces.

**Report anonymously.** We will maintain an anonymous reporting form so that anyone can flag a concern without attaching their name to it. Anonymity is respected — no exceptions.

All reports will be taken seriously. You will not be penalized for raising a concern in good faith.

---

## Enforcement

When a report is received, the organizer team will:

**Acknowledge.** You'll hear back within 48 hours confirming that your report was received and is being reviewed.

**Listen.** The organizer team will look into what happened and talk to the people involved. Both the person who reported and the person reported will have a chance to share their perspective.

**Respond.** What happens next depends on the situation. It might be a private conversation and a request to change behavior, a formal warning, a temporary step-back from spaces or events, or — in serious cases — permanent removal from the community. The response fits the situation.

**Follow up.** We'll let you know the situation was addressed. We won't share private details about the other person, but you won't be left in the dark either.

Organizers make the final call on enforcement. We'll always aim for fairness — but when fairness and safety are in tension, safety wins.

---

## A Note on Growth

Nobody is perfect. Part of being in a community is learning — sometimes learning that something you said or did caused harm you didn't intend. These guidelines are not a trap. If you make a mistake, the expectation isn't perfection — it's accountability: listen, acknowledge, do better.

We'd rather have a hard conversation and keep someone in the community than avoid the conversation and lose the trust of the people who were harmed.

---

## Scope

These guidelines apply to all PhilaCon Valley spaces, including but not limited to:

- In-person events (meetups, hackathons, workshops, social gatherings)
- Community messaging and chat platforms
- Event listing and RSVP platforms
- Social media accounts and groups operated by PhilaCon Valley
- Collaborative projects organized under PhilaCon Valley
- One-on-one interactions that occur in the context of community participation

If you're in a space that carries the PhilaCon Valley name or that exists because of this community, these guidelines apply. Behavior that occurs outside PhilaCon Valley spaces may also be considered if it directly affects the safety or wellbeing of community members.

## Changes

These guidelines are a living document. As the community grows and learns, we expect them to evolve. Significant changes will be discussed with the community before being adopted.
